# Final Business Insights Report

## Project: Customer Subscription EDA & Revenue Insights

## Executive Summary

This project analyzed customer subscription data for a fictional telecommunications company to identify churn patterns, revenue risk, and retention opportunities.

The analysis found that customer churn is both a retention issue and a revenue issue. The overall churn rate was 26.54%, but churned customers represented 30.50% of total monthly revenue. This means churned customers accounted for a larger share of revenue than their share of the customer base.

Several customer groups showed elevated churn risk, especially month-to-month customers, newer customers, fiber optic customers, electronic check customers, and high monthly charge customers. A combined high-risk segment made up of month-to-month, fiber optic, and electronic check customers had a churn rate of 60.37%, compared with 18.83% for other customers.

## Key Findings

### 1. Overall Churn Rate

The overall customer churn rate was 26.54%.

This means about one in four customers in the dataset left the company. At this level, churn represents a meaningful business problem because it can reduce recurring revenue and increase the need to replace lost customers through acquisition.

### 2. Revenue Impact of Churn

Total monthly revenue in the dataset was $456,116.60.

Churned customers represented $139,130.85 in monthly revenue, or 30.50% of total monthly revenue.

Because the revenue churn share was higher than the customer churn rate, churned customers were disproportionately tied to revenue. This suggests the company is losing higher-value customers, not just a large number of customers.

### 3. Churned Customers Had Higher Monthly Charges

Retained customers had an average monthly charge of $61.27.

Churned customers had an average monthly charge of $74.44.

This means churned customers paid about $13.17 more per month on average than retained customers. This supports the finding that churn is a recurring revenue risk.

### 4. Contract Type Was Strongly Related to Churn

Churn rate by contract type:

- Month-to-month: 42.71%
- One year: 11.27%
- Two year: 2.83%

Month-to-month customers had much higher churn than customers on longer-term contracts. This suggests that customers without long-term commitments are much more likely to leave.

### 5. Newer Customers Had Higher Churn

Churn rate by tenure group:

- 0-12 Months: 47.77%
- 13-24 Months: 28.71%
- 25-48 Months: 20.39%
- 49-72 Months: 9.51%

Churn was highest among customers in their first year and declined as tenure increased. This suggests that retention risk is highest early in the customer lifecycle.

### 6. High Monthly Charge Customers Had Higher Churn

Churn rate by monthly charge group:

- Low Monthly Charge: 10.89%
- Medium Monthly Charge: 23.94%
- High Monthly Charge: 35.36%

Customers with higher monthly charges had the highest churn rate. Since these customers contribute more monthly revenue per customer, churn in this group can have a larger financial impact.

### 7. Fiber Optic Customers Had the Highest Churn by Internet Service

Churn rate by internet service type:

- Fiber optic: 41.89%
- DSL: 18.96%
- No internet service: 7.40%

Fiber optic customers had the highest churn rate. This does not prove that fiber optic service is poor, but it does suggest the company should investigate pricing, reliability, support issues, customer expectations, and competitor pressure for this segment.

### 8. Electronic Check Customers Had the Highest Churn by Payment Method

Churn rate by payment method:

- Electronic check: 45.29%
- Mailed check: 19.11%
- Bank transfer automatic: 16.71%
- Credit card automatic: 15.24%

Electronic check customers had the highest churn rate, while customers using automatic payment methods had lower churn rates. This suggests payment method may be related to customer retention behavior.

### 9. Churn Reasons Pointed to Support and Competition

The top churn reasons included:

- Attitude of support person
- Competitor offered higher download speeds
- Competitor offered more data
- Don't know
- Competitor made better offer

These reasons suggest churn may be driven by both customer support experience and competitive pressure. Support-related churn indicates potential customer service quality issues, while competitor-related churn suggests customers may be leaving for better speed, more data, or stronger offers.

### 10. High-Risk Segment Had Very High Churn

A high-risk customer segment was created using three characteristics:

- Month-to-month contract
- Fiber optic internet service
- Electronic check payment method

This segment contained 1,307 customers and had a churn rate of 60.37%.

Other customers had a churn rate of 18.83%.

This suggests churn risk compounds when multiple high-risk characteristics appear together.

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Improve First-Year Retention

Because customers with 0-12 months of tenure had the highest churn rate, the company should focus on early customer lifecycle retention.

Recommended actions:

- Improve onboarding
- Send early engagement emails
- Offer first-year loyalty incentives
- Monitor new customer satisfaction
- Proactively contact customers showing early signs of dissatisfaction

### 2. Encourage Longer-Term Contracts

Month-to-month customers had much higher churn than customers on one-year or two-year contracts.

Recommended actions:

- Offer discounts for switching from month-to-month to one-year contracts
- Bundle services with longer-term agreements
- Promote loyalty benefits for longer commitments
- Target month-to-month customers before renewal risk increases

### 3. Investigate Fiber Optic Customer Experience

Fiber optic customers had a churn rate of 41.89%.

Recommended actions:

- Review fiber optic service reliability
- Compare fiber optic pricing against competitors
- Analyze support tickets from fiber optic customers
- Survey fiber optic customers about satisfaction and perceived value
- Monitor competitor offers related to speed and data

### 4. Reduce Electronic Check Churn Risk

Electronic check customers had the highest churn rate by payment method.

Recommended actions:

- Encourage autopay enrollment
- Offer small autopay discounts
- Improve billing communication
- Send payment experience reminders
- Investigate whether electronic check customers face more billing friction

### 5. Prioritize High Monthly Charge Customers

High monthly charge customers had elevated churn and represent more revenue per customer.

Recommended actions:

- Monitor high monthly charge customers for dissatisfaction
- Offer retention bundles or loyalty discounts
- Provide proactive customer success outreach
- Track competitor offers targeting premium customers

### 6. Target the Combined High-Risk Segment

The high-risk segment had a churn rate of 60.37%, more than three times the churn rate of other customers.

Recommended actions:

- Create a targeted retention campaign for this group
- Offer contract upgrade incentives
- Encourage automatic payment adoption
- Provide proactive support outreach
- Monitor satisfaction and usage patterns closely

### 7. Improve Customer Support Quality

The top churn reason was related to support attitude.

Recommended actions:

- Improve support training
- Track customer satisfaction after support interactions
- Review escalated support cases
- Identify teams or processes linked to poor support experiences
- Create service recovery offers for dissatisfied customers

### 8. Monitor Competitor Pressure

Several top churn reasons were competitor-related.

Recommended actions:

- Track competitor pricing, speed, data, and promotional offers
- Create win-back campaigns
- Offer targeted retention discounts when customers are at risk
- Improve value messaging around current plans

## Final Conclusion

The analysis shows that churn is concentrated among specific customer groups and has a meaningful revenue impact. The most important churn risks are tied to customer tenure, contract type, monthly charges, internet service type, payment method, support experience, and competitor pressure.

The company should prioritize retention strategies for newer customers, month-to-month customers, fiber optic customers, electronic check customers, high monthly charge customers, and customers with multiple high-risk characteristics.

The highest-priority group is the combined high-risk segment of month-to-month, fiber optic, and electronic check customers, which had a 60.37% churn rate. Targeting this group could help reduce churn and protect recurring monthly revenue.
